The GNU public license was developped out of the GNU manifesto philosophy. It is commonly referenced by free software developpers to label their work as being part of the Free Software Movement.

The Free Software Foundation is an organization dedicated to maintaining and promoting free software principles. The foundation also organizes events to further grow and build the GNU community. It is where much of the legal framework behind the GNU public license is formulated



The Bourne Again Shell is a unix terminal developped for free software use. It is commonly used in Linux systems. In fact, GNU is such a large part of the Linux operating system that the term GNU/Linux is argued as being the correct description for all Linux systems.


GIMP

The GNU Image Manipulation Program, aka GIMP, is described as a "free and open-source raster graphics editor". Though it may not have all the capabilities of something like Adobe Photoshop, it is a powerful image editor in its own right and is capable of doing industry level image manipulation.
