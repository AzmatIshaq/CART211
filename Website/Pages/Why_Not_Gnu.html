Although the GNU philosophy has noble objectives, it is clear that many commercial software companies produce highly impressive programs which can outcompete free software in regards to quality. Furthermore, the upkeep and updates for the paid software have a consistency that free software struggles to match.

Furthermore, the data indicates that people would rather use commercial operating systems. Perhaps the idea of repairing ones own system is not as enticing to average computer users as the GNU Manifesto implies.
